Hola!

Wow, look at you! You have achieved so much thanks to your effort and dedication to learning Spanish. I'm sure you can already hold long conversations, read complex articles, and give your own opinions. But you are still here because you want to continue to expand your knowledge.

You have come to the right place.

Let me tell you why!

Do you get confused when people use "Se, Me, Le, Te"? It's quite normal. Many verbs use "Se" in Spanish and it's confusing because they can be reflexive, impersonal, reciprocal, etc. But today I want to share with you other verbs that not only use "Se" but "Se + Te" or "Se + Le", which are very difficult to find in grammar books.

We will work with:

Vérsle, Dársele y Hacérsele.
These verbs have the following structure: Se + Indirect Pronoun (Me, Te, Le, Les, Nos) + Third person verb (it depends on the object if it is plural or singular).

Muchos éxitos!

Now let's talk about "Se Accidental", Which are very specific ones, and they appear when something unintentional happens or occurred naturally.
Don´t panic yet! you will see that is very easy to identify them. ;)

We will work with:

"Olvidársele, Quemársele, Rompérsele, Caérsele, Regársele", and last but not least with "Dormirse".
